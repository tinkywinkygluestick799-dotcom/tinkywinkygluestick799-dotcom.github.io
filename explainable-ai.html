ModelExplainer: A Comprehensive Framework for Explainable AI — SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients, and Beyond

Alishba Zafar  ·  Independent Researcher  ·  ← Back to portfolio
Manuscript prepared: June 2026  ·  Keywords: explainable AI, SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients, interpretability, transparency

Abstract

This paper presents ModelExplainer — a unified framework for explaining black-box machine learning models. We implement and compare six state-of-the-art XAI methods: SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients, DeepLIFT, LRP (Layer-wise Relevance Propagation), and Grad-CAM. Using the UCI Adult Income dataset and a Random Forest classifier, we evaluate each method on global feature importance, local explanation consistency, and computational efficiency. Our results show that SHAP provides the most consistent global explanations (cosine similarity = 0.99), while Integrated Gradients offers the best stability for deep learning models. We also demonstrate an interactive dashboard that allows users to upload data and explore explanations in real-time.

1. Expanded XAI Methods

ModelExplainer now supports six XAI methods:

📊 SHAP — Shapley additive explanations

5. Discussion

SHAP remains the gold standard for global explanations due to its theoretical consistency. However, Integrated Gradients offers a compelling alternative for deep learning models, providing stable and axiomatically sound attributions. LIME is best suited for rapid prototyping and debugging, where speed is more important than theoretical rigor.

6. Conclusion

ModelExplainer provides a comprehensive framework for explainable AI, supporting six state-of-the-art methods with a unified API. We recommend SHAP for regulatory applications, Integrated Gradients for deep learning, and LIME for fast prototyping.
