Quantum Error Correction and the Mathematics of Stabilizer Codes

This research investigates the error-correction performance of Shor’s 9-qubit code and Steane’s 7-qubit code under depolarizing noise. The project combines theoretical stabilizer code analysis with computational simulations implemented in Python.

Abstract

Quantum computers are highly susceptible to decoherence and noise. Quantum error correction provides a mechanism for protecting quantum information by encoding logical qubits into entangled states of multiple physical qubits. This project studies stabilizer codes and compares the performance of Shor’s and Steane’s codes under depolarizing noise.
