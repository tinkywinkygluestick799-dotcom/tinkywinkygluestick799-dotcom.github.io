NISQGuard: Error Mitigation on IBM Quantum Hardware

Alishba Zafar  |  Independent Researcher  |  ← back to portfolio
Benchmarking ZNE, PEC, and readout error mitigation on IBM Osaka.

The Problem

Today's quantum computers are too noisy. Can we reduce errors without adding extra qubits?

The Approach

We benchmark three mitigation strategies on IBM's 127-qubit Osaka device using random circuits of depths 10, 20, and 30.

Key Result

Combining ZNE with readout mitigation reduces errors by over 80% with only 3× sampling overhead.

Results

Figure 1 – Error reduction by mitigation method (Depth 30)

ZNE + readout gives the best balance of error reduction and overhead.
