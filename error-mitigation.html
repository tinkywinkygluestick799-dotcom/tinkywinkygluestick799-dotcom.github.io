🔬 Quantum Error Mitigation for Near-Term Devices

A Comprehensive Study of Noise Reduction Techniques in NISQ Quantum Computers

📋 Abstract

This research presents a comprehensive analysis of quantum error mitigation techniques for Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices. We implement and benchmark three state-of-the-art methods: Zero-Noise Extrapolation (ZNE), Probabilistic Error Cancellation (PEC), and Readout Error Mitigation. Our experiments on IBM's ibm_osaka quantum processor demonstrate that combined mitigation strategies reduce expectation value errors by up to 76% compared to unmitigated results, enabling practical quantum computations on current hardware.

📊 Experimental Results

Figure 1: Zero-Noise Extrapolation Performance

ZNE reduces error by 70-82% across all circuit depths. Exponential extrapolation slightly outperforms linear methods.

Figure 2: Error Mitigation Techniques Comparison

Combined ZNE + readout mitigation achieves the lowest error rate at 6.5%, representing an 89% reduction.

Figure 3: ZNE Error Reduction by Circuit Depth

Error reduction remains above 70% even at depth 40, demonstrating ZNE's effectiveness for deep circuits.

Figure 4: Combined Mitigation Strategy

ZNE + readout mitigation consistently outperforms either technique alone across all circuit depths.

Figure 5: Sampling Overhead vs. Error Reduction Trade-off

PEC provides the highest error reduction (87%) but at significant sampling overhead (up to 9×). ZNE offers a better balance.
